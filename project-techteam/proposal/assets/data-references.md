## Data Interpretation Notes

### Revenue Metrics

Some baseline metrics show unusual values (e.g., 402% conversion rate, very high AOV). These likely indicate:
- Data aggregation methodology differences
- Metric definition variations
- Need for validation with client

**Approach:** Use conservative baseline ($10K annual revenue) as primary reference point.

### SEO Performance Context

The performance evaluation shows a B-grade with specific characteristics:
- **Strength:** Massive visibility expansion (traffic volume)
- **Weakness:** CTR decline (traffic quality)
- **Context:** Shift from narrow focus (high engagement) to broad focus (high volume)

**Implication:** Opportunity exists for traffic quality optimization without sacrificing volume gains.

### Timeline Expectations

Based on site health indicators and theme change impact:
- **Quick Wins:** 30-90 days (5-10% improvement)
- **Optimization Phase:** 4-6 months (15-25% cumulative improvement)
- **Full Recovery:** 6-12 months (site health restored, sustainable growth)

### ROI Framework

**Source:** `06_FINANCIAL_PROJECTIONS_OUTPUT.md`

Given current baseline and data quality considerations:
- **Conservative:** 25-50% revenue improvement
- **Expected:** 75-150% revenue improvement
- **Optimistic:** 200%+ revenue improvement

**Important:** All scenarios require 6-12 month timeline due to current site health recovery needs.

## Proposal Data Usage Guidelines

### What to Use

✅ Traffic trends and patterns  
✅ SEO visibility metrics (keywords, rankings)  
✅ Site health indicators  
✅ Opportunity categories (not specific dollar amounts)  
✅ Service tier structure  
✅ Implementation timeline  
✅ Team composition and costs  

### What to Contextualize

⚠️ Specific revenue projections (data quality issues)  
⚠️ Conversion rate metrics (need validation)  
⚠️ AOV figures (unusually high)  
⚠️ Opportunity dollar amounts (use conservatively)  

### How to Present

1. **Lead with methodology**, not promises
2. **Use ranges**, not specific outcomes
3. **Emphasize trends**, not absolute numbers
4. **Focus on process**, not guaranteed results
5. **Provide context** for all metrics
