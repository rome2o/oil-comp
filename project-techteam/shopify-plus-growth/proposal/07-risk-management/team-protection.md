**Example prioritization:**

**Traditional agency model:**

**Hour cap model:**
- Incentive to be efficient (more work in fewer hours = more valuable)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è What Happens If We Approach Hour Cap?

### Mid-Month Check (Week 2)

**If trending toward cap:**
- Alert sent: "We're at 35 hours after Week 2 (54% of cap)"
- Prioritization review: "Here's what we'll complete, here's what we'll defer"
- Options presented:
  1. Continue with prioritized work only
  2. Defer low-priority tasks to next month
  3. Approve overage hours (billed separately)
  4. Upgrade to higher tier

**Example:**
> "We're at 40 hours after Week 2. We'll complete the 5 priority product pages and B2B landing page (25 more hours). The 'add reviews to 20 additional products' task (15 hours) will be deferred to Month 2. This keeps us at 65 hours total."

### End of Month (Week 4)

**If at cap:**
- "We've reached 65 hours for this month"
- All must-have work completed (product pages, commercial keywords)
- Nice-to-have work deferred to next month
- No surprise overages

**If under cap:**
- "We completed all work in 58 hours (7 hours under cap)"
- You still pay $9,500 (flat rate, not hourly)
- Extra hours can roll over to next month (if agreed)

### How We Handle Out-of-Scope Requests

**Process:**

1. **Identify:** "This request is out-of-scope - it requires 30 additional hours"
2. **Options:**
   - **Option A:** Defer to separate project (quoted separately)
   - **Option B:** Upgrade tier (if consistently out-of-scope)
   - **Option C:** Decline (if not aligned with goals)
3. **Approval:** Requires written approval before proceeding
4. **Billing:** Separate invoice or tier upgrade

**Example:**
> "You've requested professional product photography for all 80 products. This is out-of-scope (estimated 60 hours + photographer costs ~$5,000). We recommend hiring a product photographer separately. We can provide creative direction (3 hours, within cap) to ensure photos align with optimized pages."

## üéØ Prioritization Framework

### How We Decide What Gets Done First

**Within the hour cap, we prioritize by ROI:**

| Priority Level | Criteria | Examples |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| **P0 (Must Do)** | Direct revenue impact | Top 5 product page optimizations, commercial keyword targeting |
| **P1 (Should Do)** | Enables P0 work | B2B landing pages, trust signal infrastructure |
| **P2 (Nice to Have)** | Incremental improvement | Additional product reviews, minor UX tweaks |
| **P3 (Can Defer)** | Low impact | Footer redesign, about page rewrite |

**Rule:** P0 and P1 always complete. P2 and P3 only if hours remain.
