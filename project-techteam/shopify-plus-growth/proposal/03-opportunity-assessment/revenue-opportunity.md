## The Multiplier Effect: Why Dual-Track Outperforms

### Mathematical Model

```
Revenue = Sessions × Add-to-Cart Rate × Checkout Conv. × AOV

Current Baseline:
$120K = 36,490 × 2.03% × 53.3% × $441

Scenario A (Pages Only):
$207K = 36,490 × 3.5% × 53.3% × $441
(+$87K = +72%)

Scenario B (Traffic Only):
$197K = 38,000 × 3.2% × 53.3% × $441
(+$77K = +64%)

Scenario C (Both):
$285K = 39,000 × 5.0% × 53.3% × $441
(+$165K = +138%)

MULTIPLIER EFFECT:
Scenario A + B = $87K + $77K = $164K (additive thinking)
Scenario C = $165K (multiplicative reality)
```

**Why This Works:**

1. **Better traffic converts at higher rates** on any page (even unoptimized)
2. **Better pages convert at higher rates** for any traffic (even poor quality)
3. **Better traffic + Better pages = Exponential improvement**

**Real-World Example:**

- **Informational visitor** on poor page: 0.5% add-to-cart chance
- **Informational visitor** on good page: 1.5% add-to-cart chance (3x improvement)
- **Commercial visitor** on poor page: 3% add-to-cart chance (6x better than info)
- **Commercial visitor** on good page: **8-12% add-to-cart chance** (18-24x better than baseline)

This is the multiplier effect: 6x (better traffic) × 3x (better page) = **18x improvement** for the qualified segment.
