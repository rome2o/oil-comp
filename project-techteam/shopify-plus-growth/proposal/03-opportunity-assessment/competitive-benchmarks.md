### What Top Performers Do Differently

#### A. Product Page Design

**Best Practice (Top 20% of E-Commerce):**

**Above-the-Fold (First Screen):**
- Large product image (70% of screen)
- Clear headline (benefit-driven, not feature-driven)
- Star rating + review count (social proof immediately visible)
- Add-to-cart button (bright color, large touch target)
- Trust badges (GC/MS, organic, made in USA)

**Below-the-Fold (Scroll 1-2):**
- Detailed product description (benefits first, then ingredients)
- Customer reviews (5-10 visible, with photos)
- Usage instructions (how-to, tips, recipes)
- Trust signals (certifications, lab reports, guarantees)

**HBNO Current State:**
- âŒ Generic headline ("100% Pure Rosemary Essential Oil")

#### C. Mobile Optimization

**Best Practice (Mobile-First Leaders):**

**Layout:**
- Vertical scroll (not horizontal swipe for key actions)
- Sticky add-to-cart button (always visible)
- Progressive disclosure (accordion sections, not all content at once)
- Large touch targets (minimum 48px Ã— 48px)

**Performance:**
- Page load <3 seconds (mobile networks)
- Lazy loading images (loads as you scroll)
- Minified CSS/JS (smaller file sizes)
- WebP image format (30-50% smaller than JPEG)

**Content:**
- Shorter paragraphs (3-4 lines max on mobile)
- Scannable bullets (not dense prose)
- Video demonstrations (easier to consume on mobile)
- Simplified navigation (hamburger menu, not full nav)

**HBNO Current State:**
- âŒ Desktop layout on mobile (not mobile-first)
- âŒ 4.2 second load time (above 3 second threshold)
- âŒ Small buttons (hard to tap)
- âŒ Dense text (desktop copy on mobile)

**Gap:** HBNO is 3-5 years behind mobile-first best practices.
