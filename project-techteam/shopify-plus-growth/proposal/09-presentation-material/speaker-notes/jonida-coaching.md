## üé§ How to Present to Jonida

### Opening (Set the Frame)

> "Jonida, I know you've been frustrated watching traffic grow while conversions decline. I wanted to show you what we found in our analysis and how we can fix this.
>
> Today I'm going to walk you through:
> 1. Exactly what went wrong with the Upwork approach
> 2. Why it happened (the data tells a clear story)
> 3. How to fix it in 90 days
> 4. What it'll cost and what you'll get back
>
> My goal is to give you everything you need to take this to Josef with confidence. Sound good?"

**Why this works:** Positions you as helping HER succeed, not just selling a service.

---

### Throughout the Presentation

**Slide 1-3 (Problem):**
- Focus on validation: "You've been seeing this"
- Acknowledge her observations: "This validates what you've been noticing"
- Build trust: "The data confirms your instinct"

**Slide 4-6 (Diagnosis):**
- Focus on expertise: "This is why data-driven strategy matters"
- Show differentiation: "This is what the Upwork team missed"
- Position her intelligence: "You knew something was off"

**Slide 7-9 (Solution):**
- Focus on completeness: "This addresses everything you've been seeing"
- Show proof: "This isn't hope and prayer‚Äîit's proven methodology"
- Build confidence: "This is how we fix what Upwork broke"

**Slide 10-12 (Plan & ROI):**
- Focus on clarity: "Each month has clear deliverables"
- Show measurability: "You'll see progress every week"
- Emphasize transparency: "Full visibility into what's working"

**Slide 13-15 (Protection & Close):**
- Focus on safety: "You're protected with hard caps and no lock-in"
- Show ease: "Minimal time commitment from your team"
- Position her as hero: "This is the strategic solution Josef needs"

---

### Closing with Jonida

**If Josef is NOT on the call:**

> "Jonida, based on what you've seen, does this make sense as the approach to fix the traffic and conversion issues?
>
> [Wait for her response]
>
> Great. Here's what I recommend: Let's schedule a follow-up call with Josef in the next 2-3 days. I'll present the same deck, but you can frame it as: 'I asked [your name] to analyze our situation and propose a fix. Here's what they found.'
>
> That way, you're bringing Josef the solution, and I'm the execution partner. You're the hero, I'm the support. Sound good?
>
> In the meantime, I'll send you the full proposal package so you have all the data to discuss with Josef before the call. What questions do you have right now?"

**If Josef IS on the call:**

> "Jonida, does this approach make sense to you?"
>
> [Let her respond first]
>
> "Josef, what questions do you have?"
>
> [Address Josef's questions, then close]

**Why this works:** Jonida champions it first, then Josef sees she supports it. Social proof.

> "I totally understand. You were burned by Upwork's 'trust me' approach. That's why everything we do is data-driven and A/B tested. You'll see the data every week‚Äîno blind trust required. If we're not hitting milestones by Month 2, you can cancel. You're in control."

### Red Flag #2: Jonida says "I'll think about it"

**What it means:** She's unsure how to position this to Josef  
**How to address:**
> "Let me ask: What specifically are you thinking about? Is it:

**How to address:**
> "You'll have direct access to me throughout the project. Weekly calls, email anytime, Slack if you prefer. Response time: within 4 hours during business hours. You won't be ghosted like you were with Upwork freelancers."
