## ğŸ¯ Negotiation Tactics

### Tactic #1: The Anchor

**When to use:** Early in pricing conversation

**How:**
1. Present Plus Tier ($15,000/month) first
2. Legitimize it ("This is for urgent turnarounds")
3. Show full value ($17,500 with free audit)

**Why it works:**
- Makes $9,500 feel like a bargain
- Establishes high reference point
- Positions Growth as "smart middle choice"

---

### Tactic #2: The Discount (Waive Audit)

**When to use:** When presenting Growth Tier pricing

**How:**
1. Mention audit fee ($2,500) is normally separate
2. Pause slightly
3. "But given your situation, I'm going to waive that $2,500 audit fee"
4. Emphasize: "You're getting $12,000 worth of value for $9,500/month"

**Why it works:**
- Josef "wins" $2,500 without negotiating
- Satisfies wholesaler psychology
- Creates goodwill and reciprocity

---

### Tactic #3: Current Spend Comparison

**When to use:** When they resist price

**How:**
1. Remind: "You're currently spending $10,000/month on Upwork"
2. Contrast: "This is $9,500/month"
3. Reframe: "That's $500 LESS per month"
4. Add: "But instead of going backwards (-44% conversions), you go forward (+146% add-to-cart)"

**Why it works:**
- Reframes from "expense" to "savings"
- Makes them realize they're already spending more for worse results
- Removes price objection

---

### Tactic #4: Opportunity Cost Calculation

**When to use:** When they want to delay or start small

**How:**
1. Show monthly lost opportunity: "$165,000/month"
2. Calculate delay cost: "3 months delay = $495,000 lost"
3. Compare to service cost: "$9,500/month vs $165,000/month opportunity"
4. Ask: "Which number is bigger?"

**Why it works:**
- Makes delay/cheap options look expensive
- Creates urgency without pressure
- Quantifies the cost of inaction

---

### Tactic #5: Risk Reversal

**When to use:** When they're hesitant due to Upwork experience

**How:**
1. Acknowledge fear: "You were burned by Upwork. I get it."
2. Show protection:
   - Month-to-month (no lock-in)
   - Hard hour caps (no overages)
   - Transparent reporting (full visibility)
   - A/B testing (only keep what works)
3. Position cancellation: "If we're not delivering by Month 2, cancel. You're in control."

**Why it works:**
- Removes perceived risk
- Builds trust through transparency
- Makes commitment feel safe

---

### Tactic #6: The Isolation Close

**When to use:** When they say "we need to think about it"

**How:**
1. Ask: "What specifically do you need to think about?"
2. List options: "Is it budget? Timeline? Trust? Something else?"
3. Isolate one concern
4. Address that specific concern
5. Ask: "If we solve [concern], are you ready to move forward?"

**Why it works:**
- Turns vague hesitation into specific objection
- Allows you to address real blocker
- Moves them toward decision
