## üîß ADDITIONAL FLAW: Pricing Model Confusion

**Date:** November 14, 2025  
**Issue:** Mixing outcome-based and hour-based pricing creates confusion  
**Impact:** Client confusion about what they're paying for, weakened value proposition

---

### The Problem with Current Pricing

**We're Sending Mixed Messages:**

1. **We claim it's "outcome-based"** ‚Üí Focused on results (3.5% A2C in 90 days)
2. **But we're selling hour-based tiers** ‚Üí 15 hrs/week vs 20 hrs/week vs 30 hrs/week
3. **We present "hard hour caps" as a benefit** ‚Üí Protection from scope creep

**This creates confusion:** If clients are paying for outcomes, why do hours matter?

---

### What "Hard Hour Cap" Actually Means

A **hard hour cap** means:
- We will **not exceed** a specific number of billable hours per week
- Client knows their **maximum possible cost** upfront
- Protects against **scope creep** ("while you're at it, can you also...")

**Why It's Beneficial to Clients:**
- **Budget certainty** - No surprise invoices
- **Forces prioritization** - We focus on highest-impact work only
- **Prevents waste** - We can't pad hours or gold-plate solutions

---

### The Real Issue: Mixing Two Pricing Models

**Current proposal tries to combine:**
1. **Outcome-based pricing** (we'll get you to 3.5% A2C)
2. **Time-based pricing** ($9,500/month = 20 hrs/week)
3. **Hard caps as "protection"** (but protection from what if it's outcome-based?)

**This Creates Questions:**
- If Growth tier gets 3.5% A2C in 90 days with 20 hrs/week...
- And Starter tier gets 3.0% A2C in 90 days with 15 hrs/week...
- **What's the client actually paying for?** The outcome or the hours?

---

**Why Amount of Hours Differs Between Tiers**

**The Logic Should Be:**
**More hours = More work done in parallel = Faster/better results**

**Example:**
- **Starter (15 hrs/week):** Optimize 4-6 product pages/month + basic SEO cleanup
- **Growth (20 hrs/week):** Optimize 8-12 product pages/month + comprehensive SEO + A/B testing
- **Plus (30 hrs/week):** Optimize 14-20 product pages/month + advanced SEO + aggressive testing + category pages

**The outcome difference is driven by scope, not just time.**

---

### ‚úÖ Solution: Reframe as Deliverable-Based Tiers

**Remove all hour references from client-facing materials.**

Instead of:
> "Growth Tier: $11,500/month, 20 hrs/week, 3.5% A2C target"

Say:
> "Growth Tier: $11,500/month
> - 8-12 product pages/month optimized with conversion-focused designs
> - Comprehensive SEO cleanup (toxic backlink disavowal + content strategy)
> - A/B testing on key conversion points
> - Bi-weekly performance reporting
> - **Target:** 3.5% A2C by Month 3, 4.5% by Month 6"

**The "hard cap" becomes internal:**
- We budget 20 hrs/week internally to deliver the outcome
- Client never sees this‚Äîthey just see the deliverable targets
- If it takes us 15 hours or 25 hours some weeks, client doesn't care
