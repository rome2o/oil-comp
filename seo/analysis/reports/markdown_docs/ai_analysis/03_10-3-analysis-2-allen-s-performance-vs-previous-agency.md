This report provides an objective evaluation of the company's in-house SEO efforts managed by Allen, specifically for the period prior to the recent website theme change. This isolates the impact of his strategic and tactical execution compared to the performance of the previous external agency.

1. Overall Performance Assessment¬∂Grade: B
Allen has made a significant and positive impact in his first five months. He successfully executed a foundational strategy focused on dramatically increasing the website's overall visibility and traffic, achieving results that far surpass the previous agency in top-line metrics.
The strategy has shifted from a narrow, high-engagement approach (agency) to a broad-net, high-volume approach (Allen). This has resulted in a massive 222% increase in average daily clicks and a staggering 627% increase in daily impressions. His work has also improved the site's average ranking position by nearly 20%.
The grade is a "B" and not an "A" due to the severe 78% decline in Click-Through Rate (CTR). While the traffic gains are impressive, the low CTR indicates a significant opportunity to improve targeting, messaging, and user engagement, which is the critical next step in maturing the SEO program.

2. Strengths¬∂Allen has demonstrated clear strengths in foundational and growth-focused SEO tactics.

Massive Visibility Expansion: The 627% increase in impressions is the standout metric. This indicates Allen has been highly effective at technical SEO, on-page optimization, and expanding keyword targeting. The website is now visible for a much wider array of search queries than ever before, building a strong foundation for future growth.
Effective Traffic Generation: The primary goal of SEO is to drive qualified traffic. An increase of 222% in daily clicks is an exceptional achievement in just five months. He has successfully translated expanded visibility into tangible visits.
Consistent Ranking Improvement: Lowering the average position from 34 to 28 is a difficult task. This shows that his optimizations are being rewarded by search engines and the site's overall authority is likely improving. He is successfully moving the needle across a large set of keywords.

3. Areas for Improvement¬∂The data points clearly to one primary area for strategic refinement.

Critically Low Click-Through Rate (CTR): The drop from 5.47% to 1.20% is the most significant concern. This suggests a potential disconnect between the search queries the site is ranking for and the value proposition presented in the search results.
Possible Cause: The broad-net strategy is likely targeting many top-of-funnel, informational keywords where user intent is not commercial.
Possible Cause: Title tags and meta descriptions may not be compelling enough to entice clicks, even when the site ranks for relevant terms.

Traffic Quality vs. Quantity: While traffic volume is high, the low CTR may be an early indicator that the new traffic is less qualified than the traffic generated by the agency's hyper-focused strategy. We need to analyze conversion data (e.g., quote requests, contact form submissions) from this new traffic to understand its business value.

Impressions & Clicks: The growth Allen achieved (+627% impressions, +222% clicks) is well above industry benchmarks. A typical "good" year-over-year growth target might be 20-30%. Allen achieved this in five months, which is exceptional.
Average Position: An average position of ~28 is not uncommon for a B2B site targeting a wide range of industry terms. The key is the improvement of 18.54%, which is a strong positive signal.
Click-Through Rate (CTR): A CTR of 1.20% is below the industry average. While B2B CTRs are generally lower than B2C, a healthy average for non-branded terms often falls in the 2-4% range.
